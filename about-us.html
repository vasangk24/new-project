Problem Statements

Maritime Automation

Design and prototyping smart marine operation system. With the advancement in technology and application of emerging technologies in maritime sector is moving toward automation to improve safety, efficiency, and reduce manpower dependency. However, challenges remain in ensuring operations in complex environments like congested ports or during adverse weather. The challenge focus on demonstration of suitable automation solution to the maritime industry.

Environmental Sustainability

The maritime industry is essential for global trade but contributes significantly to environmental issues such as greenhouse gas emissions, marine pollution, and biodiversity loss. Participants are challenged to design innovative, data- driven, and scalable solutions that promote environmental sustainability across maritime operations. Solutions should aim to reduce ecological impact while maintaining operational efficiency. The key focus areas are Emission Reduction and Decarbonization, Marine Pollution Monitoring and Prevention, Sustainable Port Operations, Ecosystem Protection and Biodiversity etc.

Marine IoT and AI

The maritime industry is undergoing a digital transformation to meet environmental, operational, and safety demands. Participants are challenged to develop intelligent and connected solutions using IoT (Internet of Things) and AI (Artificial Intelligence) to solve real-world challenges in marine environmental sustainability, vessel operations, and ocean monitoring. The challenge focusses to leverage sensor data, edge computing, AI/ML algorithms, and predictive analytics to create a prototype that improves efficiency, safety, and ecological balance in marine environments.

Block Chain

Global maritime trade involves complex, multi-party transactions that are often paper-based, opaque, and prone to fraud, delays, and inefficiencies. Blockchain offers a transformative opportunity to streamline operations, enhance transparency, ensure data integrity, and improve trust among stakeholders in the maritime ecosystem. The challenge is to design a blockchain-based solution that addresses a critical problem in maritime logistics, port operations, vessel tracking, compliance, or sustainability by creating a tamper-proof, decentralized system for secure data sharing and verification.

Underwater Vehicles

The ocean remains one of the least explored and most fragile environments on Earth. Autonomous and remotely operated underwater vehicles (AUVs/ROVs) are revolutionizing the way we monitor marine ecosystems, support maritime operations, and respond to underwater challenges. The task is to design, prototype, or simulate a solution involving underwater vehicles that addresses a critical need in ocean research, marine sustainability, offshore operations, or underwater infrastructure monitoring. Participants are encouraged to integrate AI, sensor data, real-time control, and communications to enable smarter, more capable underwater systems.

Simulation & VR

The maritime industry is embracing simulation and virtual reality (VR) to improve training, safety, operations, and sustainability. With growing complexity in ship navigation, port operations, and offshore activities, realistic and interactive simulation environments are vital for preparing crews, reducing risks, and enhancing decision-making. The goal is to create an immersive, interactive, and scalable experience that provides realistic training, scenario planning, or operational insight.

Innovative alternative marine applications

The ocean holds vast potential not just for transportation and trade, but also for new, unconventional, and high-impact innovations that can transform how we live, work, and interact with marine environments. This challenge invites you to reimagine the use of marine spaces, vessels, and technology by developing non-traditional, future-focused solutions that leverage the ocean in sustainable, socially beneficial, or technologically novel ways. Participants are encouraged to explore cross-disciplinary ideas combining marine science, renewable energy, biotechnology, circular economy, and more.

Inland Waterways

Inland waterways offer a sustainable, cost-effective, and underutilized mode of transport for cargo and passengers. However, challenges such as navigation safety, cargo tracking, environmental impact, intermodal integration, and infrastructure management hinder their full potential. The focus of the challenge is to develop innovative digital, hardware, or policy-enabled solutions that can help modernize and optimize the use of rivers, canals, and inland ports for sustainable transport, efficient logistics, and improved safety.
